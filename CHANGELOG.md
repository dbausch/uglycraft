# CHANGELOG — UGLYCRAFT

What's new in **UGLYCRAFT**, the modern remake. (The original 1996 DOS game and
its Linux port have their own changelog in
[`original/CHANGELOG.md`](original/CHANGELOG.md).)

---

## [Unreleased]

The big one. Version 1.5 was the ten-level first half of the game. Since then
UGLYCRAFT has **doubled in size** and gained a whole second act.

### The headline: Act 2 — ten brand-new levels (11–20)

A new run of vault levels that are **randomly generated** — the rooms, treasure,
puzzles and enemies are laid out fresh every time, so Act 2 never plays out quite
the same way twice. What you'll get up to:

- **Explore sprawling vaults** made of many connected rooms, instead of a single
  screen per level.
- **Collect materials and craft** your way through — build stone walls to fence
  off enemies, and lay planks into bridges to cross water.
- **Find keys and open locked doors** — seven colours of key guard the good loot.
- **Solve block-pushing puzzles** — shove heavy blocks onto pressure plates to
  swing gates open (push carelessly and a block wedged in a dead end blows up,
  costing you points).
- **Survive new hazards** — deep water you have to bridge, and flame jets that
  flare up on a rhythm. Grab a shield and you can walk through the fire while it
  lasts.
- **Outwit new enemies** — guards that patrol fixed routes, and a hulking forge
  ogre (from level 16 on) that smashes straight through walls you've built.

### Changes you'll feel everywhere — Act 1 included

- **Grab the loot, then get out.** Levels no longer end the instant you snatch
  the last treasure. Now an exit door unlocks and you have to make it out —
  enemies and all — so every level ends on a getaway instead of a freeze-frame.
- **Clearer feedback.** A distinct sound plays whenever a move isn't allowed
  (like trying to build somewhere you can't), so you're never left wondering why
  nothing happened.
- The first ten levels were given proper entrances and tidied-up starting spots
  to fit the new exit-the-door flow.

### Fair, and always beatable

Because Act 2's levels are built on the fly, a lot of care went into making sure
every single one is **fair and completable**: treasure, keys and puzzles are
always reachable, nothing ever spawns somewhere you can't get to, and no random
layout can trap you. Under the hood the game was largely rebuilt to make all of
this possible and to run smoothly — but as a player you should just find it
solid and dependable.

### What to expect

Roughly twice the game. The familiar chase-and-grab arcade action of Act 1 is
untouched — it now simply leads into a deeper, puzzle-driven second act full of
crafting, keys, hazards and freshly built vaults that reward exploration and play
out differently every run.

## [1.5] – 2026-06-21

The last release before this update: the complete first half of UGLYCRAFT. Ten
hand-built levels of arcade chase-and-collect, with tougher, smarter enemies on
Hard and a boss on level 10; a shield power-up; scoring, lives and high scores;
original sound effects and a different music track for every level; and a title
screen telling the history of UGLI. Earlier versions (0.5 through 1.5) are
recorded in the project's release tags.
